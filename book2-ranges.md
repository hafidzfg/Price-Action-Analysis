# Book 2: Trading Price Action Trading Ranges — Compact Reference

> Al Brooks, "Trading Price Action Trading Ranges" (2012, Wiley). Brooks terminology only.

## Quick Reference

- Markets spend ~70% of time in TRs. Most breakouts fail (~80%).
- Tight TRs: 51-55% probability ceiling. Never enter on stops.
- H2 in trend = continuation. H2 in range = reversal. Context is everything.
- DT/DB/H&S are continuation patterns, not reversals (Brooks context).
- Barbwire: never enter on breakout, fade the breakout trend bar.
- Gaps are magnets — market tends to test/fill them.
- Trader's Equation: prob × reward > (1 − prob) × risk.

---

## 1. Breakout Framework

### Entry Methods (worst → best)
1. Breakout entry (1 tick beyond swing H/L) — worst R:R
2. Market entry on close of breakout bar — risky
3. **Pullback entry (H1/H2 or L1/L2) — BEST**
4. If ≥3 strong trend bars: buy small pullback immediately

### Stop & Target
- **Stop:** 1 tick below lowest bar in bull spike (above highest in bear spike)
- **Target:** measured move = height of breakout spike (≥60% prob, 80% on strong BO)

### Breakout Signals

| Will Succeed | Will Fail |
|-------------|-----------|
| Large body, small/no tails, vol 10-20× | Small body, large tail |
| Breaks multiple resistance levels | Next bar: reversal/inside bar |
| Urgency, 2-3 follow-through bars | Pullbacks >⅔ of bar height while forming |
| Spike grows 5-10 bars, micro gaps | Pullback falls below BO point |
| Growing buying/selling pressure in range | BO buyer can't profit before pullback |
| First pullback after ≥3 BO bars, brief | Spike breaks resistance by 1 tick then reverses |

**Real BOs feel unclear and scary.** Easy setups → small fast scalps.

---

## 2. Breakout Pullback (High-Reliability Setup)

Every BO pulls back → that pullback = initial failed BO. Two paths:
1. Failure succeeds → **TREND REVERSAL**
2. Failure fails → **BREAKOUT PULLBACK** (trade this)

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Entry | After bull BO, pullback tests BO area → buy 1 tick above signal bar high |
| Breakout test | Pullback within 1-2 ticks of entry = very reliable |
| Stop | Below pullback signal bar low |
| Target | At least 2 legs up (BO = leg 1, continuation = leg 2) |

---

## 3. Breakout Entries in Strong Trends

**Entry quality (best → worst):** Limit orders below prior bar low → H1/L1 → H2/L2 → buy stop above spike high → BO entry above prior swing high.

**Rules:** Stop = below recent minor swing low. Target = measured move from spike. In strong trend: any entry works. **Pullbacks > breakouts** for R:R.

---

## 4. Gaps

| Type | When | Action |
|------|------|--------|
| Breakout/Breakaway | Start of trend | Ride — strength |
| Measuring | Middle of trend | Take partials at measured move |
| Exhaustion | End of trend | Fade/reverse |
| Ordinary | Within spike/range | Context |

**Key rules:**
- All trend bars ARE gaps. Every strong trend bar = price gap.
- **Micro measuring gap:** 3 trending bars, bar 3 doesn't overlap bar 1. Pullback test holds → entry.
- **MA gap bar:** Bar entirely on other side of MA. Entry: stop 1 tick beyond gap bar extreme. Target: test of trend extreme.
- **Exhaustion gap:** After 10-20+ bar trend, unusually large trend bar. Take profits / fade.
- **Gaps are magnets** — market tends to test/fill. In strong trend, may not fill for long time.

---

## 5. Magnets: S/R Levels

### Measured Move Types
| Type | Method | Target |
|------|--------|--------|
| Leg 1 = Leg 2 | Spike → pullback → leg 2 ≈ same height | Spike height projected from end |
| Spike & Channel | Channel height ≈ spike height | Spike height projected from end |
| TR Middle | Strong move → TR → midpoint projects BO | Start to midline, projected equidistantly |
| Measuring Gap | Middle of gap ≈ middle of trend leg | Middle_of_gap + (Middle_of_gap − Start_of_leg) |

### Complete Magnet List
Trend lines, channel lines, measured moves, spike & channel start, prior day H/L/O/C, swing highs/lows, BO points, gaps (any kind), trend extremes, TR extremes + middle, final flags, barbwire, entry/signal bar stops, huge trend bar opposite extreme, daily/weekly/monthly S/R, round numbers.

**Rules:** Trade with-trend toward magnet until tested. Countertrend only with prior TL break + clear reversal. Expect 1-5 tick overshoot at strong magnets.

## 6. First Pullback Sequence (Trend Weakening)

Each first occurrence = with-trend entry until major TL break.

| # | Sign (Bull → Bear) |
|---|-------------------|
| 1-4 | Bodies smaller → tails on tops → more overlap → doji |
| 5-7 | Bear body → lower highs → lower lows |
| 8 | **One-legged pullback** (H1) |
| 9 | **Two-legged pullback** (H2, 5-10 bars) |
| 10 | **Three-legged pullback** (Wedge bull flag, 5-15 bars) |
| 11-12 | Minor TL break → bar touches MA |
| 13-15 | Rally has bear bars → close below MA → **high below MA** (MA gap bar) |
| 16-17 | Major TL break → second leg down before recovery |
| 18-21 | Rally has ≥2 pullbacks with bear bodies → larger 2-legged pullback → TR → larger TR |

(Bear → Bull: mirror.) **After multiple winners of same type → WAIT.**

## 7. Double Tops & Bottoms

| Pattern | Context | Entry | Stop | Target |
|---------|---------|-------|------|--------|
| DB bull flag | Bull trend, 2 legs ~same price | Buy 1 tick above 2nd bottom reversal bar | Below DB low | Measured move up |
| DT bear flag | Bear trend, mirror | Short 1 tick below 2nd top reversal bar | Above DT high | Measured move down |
| Failed DB → wedge | DB fails, BO fails 1-2 bars | Buy reversal from failed BO | Below wedge extreme | Measured move |
| Failed DT → wedge | Mirror | Short reversal from failed BO | Above wedge extreme | Measured move |
| DB after climax | Sell climax → higher low | Buy strong bull reversal bar | Below DB low | Swing — often low of day |

---

## 8. 20 Gap Bars & MA Gap Bars

**20 Gap Bars:** 20+ bars without touching MA. First MA touch = high-prob setup. Entry: stop 1 tick beyond signal bar. Swing trade. After climax → expect 2-legged correction first. After failed 1st entry → take 2nd, NOT 3rd.

**MA Gap Bar:** Bar entirely on other side of MA (bull: high below MA). Deeper than 20 gap bar touch. Entry: 1 tick beyond gap bar extreme. Swing to test trend extreme. Second MA gap bar entry more reliable.

---

## 9. Bar Counting in Ranges

| Pattern | Trade When | Do NOT Trade When |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| H1/L1 | Trend spike phase, no climax | TR extreme, climax |
| H2/L2 in trend | Continuation near MA | Opposite context |
| **H2/L2 in TR** | **Range edge (H2 at bottom, L2 at top)** | **Above/below MA in range middle** |
| H3/L3 | With-trend wedge flag | Strong opp. momentum from failed H2/L2 |

**Key:** In trend, H2 = continuation (buy near high). In TR, H2 = reversal (buy near bottom). **Avoid** H2 above MA in range.

**Failed H2 evolution:** Weak momentum → expect H3 wedge (buy). Strong bear breakout → wait for H4 or skip.

---

## 10. Wedge Pullback vs Reversal

| Type | Entry | Stop | Target |
|------|-------|------|--------|
| **Wedge pullback** (with-trend, 3 countertrend pushes) | First entry OK | Beyond wedge extreme | Start of channel or measured move |
| **Wedge reversal** (countertrend, ≥20 bars, clear TL) | **Wait for second entry** | Beyond reversal extreme | Measured move |

**Most wedges = continuation patterns, not reversals.**

## 11. H&S (Brooks Context)

| Pattern | Interpretation | Trade |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| H&S top in bull trend | Wedge bull flag (continuation) | **BUY** failed neckline breakdown |
| H&S top in bear trend | Wedge bear flag | Short neckline breakdown (with-trend) |
| DT in bull trend | Continuation (bull flag) | Short only with extra bearish evidence |
| DB in bull trend | Often H2 buy setup | Long with bullish context |

## 12. Trading Range Core

### Identification
Sideways between S/R, multiple reversals. 4-5 reversals in first hour → range day. Rallies to top appear like BO → **80% fail**.

### Entries
| Setup | Entry | Stop | Target |
|-------|-------|------|--------|
| H2 near range bottom (DB) | Buy 1 tick above signal bar | Below range low | Opposite side |
| L2 near range top (DT) | Short 1 tick below signal bar | Above range high | Opposite side |
| Wedge bull flag near bottom | Buy above signal bar | Below wedge extreme | Opposite side |
| Failed BO trap | Fade opposite 1 tick beyond BO bar | Beyond BO bar extreme | Opposite side |

### Rules
- Risk ≥ reward. Target: opposite side (scalp).
- After 5-10 bars rallying → only shorts. After 5-10 bars falling → only longs.
- Rarely enter on stops in middle of range.
- **Failed BO traps:** Strong BO bar reverses 1-3 bars → fade. But **2+ consecutive trend bars** → BO likely real, don't fade.

---

## 13. Tight TR / Barbwire

### Cardinal Rule
> **A tight TR trumps everything.** BO probability never >~55%.

### Rules
| Do NOT | Do |
|--------|-----|
| Enter on stops in tight TR | Wait for strong trend bar to break out |
| Enter on BOs (most fail) | 2+ consecutive trend bars → wait for BO pullback → enter with-trend |
| Buy below MA / sell above MA | 1 trend bar → fade it |

**Fade logic:** Short 1 tick below BO bar low (bull BO fails). If short fails → go long 1 tick above = BO pullback buy.

**Barbwire:** 3+ overlapping bars with doji. Walk away or fade BO. Near MA: break away from MA direction.

---

## 14. Triangles

TR with 3+ pushes. All types (ascending, descending, symmetrical, expanding) trade the same.

- **Trade edges:** Buy near bottom, short near top (if tall enough).
- **Trade BO:** Don't enter on first BO. Strong (2+ trend bars) → wait for BO pullback. Weak (1 bar) → fade.
- **Expanding triangle:** Short top, buy bottom. Hard to predict.

## 15. Key Inflection Times

| Time (PST) | Event | Trade |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| 11:00-11:30 | Stop run (strong trend days) | After stop run fails → enter with trend |
| 11:00-11:30 | TR day: sharp move to opposite extreme | BO often fails |
| 7:00/7:30 | Economic reports | Context: 1-bar sell-off in bull open → buy; multi-bar bear spike → short |

## 16. Counting Legs

- Trends & countertrends tend to have **two legs**. Spike & channel = inherently two-legged.
- After strong 1st leg → pullback → **2nd leg** (with-trend entry).
- After 1st countertrend leg → do NOT enter against → expect 2nd leg.
- **Two-legged rally to MA in bear trend** → L2 short at MA.
- **Perfect BO test** (within 1 tick of key level) → high odds measured move opposite.

---

## 17. Need Two Reasons

Always have ≥2 reasons before entering.

**Valid:** Signal bar, MA pullback (H2/L2), BO pullback, pullback in always-in market, S/R test, dueling lines, H2/L2 in context, DB/DT, wedge/final flag, any failure, second entry.

**One reason only:** (1) Strong trend (2) TL overshoot + good reversal bar (3) Second entry.

---

## 18. Trader's Equation

> `prob × reward > (1 − prob) × risk`

| Win Rate | Min Reward | Setups |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| 70%+ | ≥ 0.5× risk | High-prob scalps (rare) |
| 60%+ | ≥ risk (prefer 2×) | Pullbacks in trends, BO pullbacks |
| ~50% | ≥ 2× risk | TR entries, fading BOs |
| ≤40% | ≥ 2-3× risk | Reversal tops/bottoms |

Confident → 60%. Uncertain → 50% — don't take unless reward >> risk. **Never** reward < risk unless 80%+ win rate.

---

## 19. Scalp vs Swing

| Type | Hold | Profit | Prob | When |
|------|------|--------|------|------|
| Scalp | 1-15 min | ≈ risk | 67%+ needed | Obvious, high-prob |
| Swing | 15 min–full day | ≥ 2× risk | 40-50% | Unclear/scary |

Obvious → scalp. Unclear/scary → swing. Best: scalp part + swing balance with BE stop.

---

## 20. Entry Methods & Stops

**Stop vs Limit:**
| Situation | Entry |
|-----------|-------|
| Strong spike | Stop (direction) or Market |
| Channel (weak trend) | Limit (fade extremes) or Stop (pullbacks) |
| TR day | Limit (buy low, sell high) |
| Pullback in trend | Stop (confirmation) |
| Beginners | Stop only |

**Stops:** Standard = 1 tick beyond signal bar. After strong entry bar → tighten to 1 tick beyond entry bar. Doji entry bar → keep original. Trailing in trends: below/above prior swing. Halfway to target → breakeven. **TR: do NOT trail** — take partials.

---

## 21. Getting Trapped

| Trap | Trade |
|------|-------|
| Weak L1/L2 in strong bull trend | Buy below on limit (bear trap) |
| Weak H1/H2 in strong bear trend | Short above on limit (bull trap) |
| Failed L2/H2 | Reverse (expect 2+ legs) |
| First BO below bull micro channel | Usually fails → buy |
| First BO above bear micro channel | Usually fails → short |
| Strong BO bars at TR extremes | Often exhaustion → fade |
| L2 after multiple sell climaxes | Likely trap → buy |

Tight trap stops (2-4 ticks) work — reversal is sharp. Trapped out → re-enter. Trapped in → exit immediately.

## 22. Decision Flowchart

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Strong BO, 3+ trend bars, small tails | LONG/SHORT at market or tiny pullback |
| BO bar large, next bar reversal/inside | WAIT (may fail) |
| Pullback after BO: weak reversal opp. direction | LONG/SHORT (BO pullback) |
| Pullback after BO: strong reversal pullback direction | FORGET BO, reverse |
| Trend 10+ bars + huge climax | Exhaustion fade |
| Range rally to top (5-10 bars) | Only shorts |
| Range sell-off to bottom (5-10 bars) | Only longs |
| Tight TR / barbwire | WAIT |
| H2 in bull trend at MA | LONG |
| H2 in TR above MA | Fade with SHORT limit |
| L2 in bear trend at MA | SHORT |
| L2 in TR below MA | Fade with LONG limit |
| Failed H2 + weak momentum | Expect H3 wedge — buy |
| Failed H2 + strong bear BO | Wait H4 or skip |
| Strong bull BO in TR → 1 bar only | SHORT 1 tick below BO bar low |
| Strong bear BO in TR → 1 bar only | LONG 1 tick above BO bar high |
| BO in TR with 2+ trend bars | WAIT for BO pullback — with-trend |
| First MA touch after 20+ gap bars | LONG/SHORT with PA entry |
| First MA gap bar | LONG/SHORT (swing) |
| 11:00-11:30 PST stop run fails | Enter with trend |

## 23. Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Barbwire | 3+ overlapping doji bars = tight TR, avoid |
| Breakout pullback | Retracement 1-5 bars after BO |
| Breakout test | Price approaches BO point from opposite side |
| DB bull flag | 2-legged pullback in bull trend, both legs ~same price |
| DT bear flag | 2-legged rally in bear trend, both legs ~same price |
| Fade | Enter opposite direction |
| Failed failure | Failed BO that fails again → strong 2nd signal |
| Final flag | Last pullback before trend reversal |
| Gap | Area between bars with no overlap; all trend bars = gaps |
| MA gap bar | Bar entirely on opposite side of MA |
| Measuring gap | Mid-trend gap projecting measured move |
| Exhaustion gap | Late-trend gap, often reversal |
| Micro measuring gap | 3-bar: bars 1 & 3 don't overlap |
| Trader's Equation | prob × reward > (1 − prob) × risk |
| Trap | Stop-run out of good trade or into bad one |
| Wedge bull flag | 3-push down in bull trend (continuation) |
| Wedge bear flag | 3-push up in bear trend (continuation) |
| Wedge reversal | 3+ push ending trend (countertrend) |

| Always-In | Direction if forced long or short now |
| Spike & Channel | Spike (momentum) → channel (two-sided) → often TR |
