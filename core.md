# Price Action Core — Universal Reference

> Al Brooks, "Trading Price Action" series (Wiley). Applies in ALL market conditions — trends, ranges, reversals. Brooks terminology only.

## Quick Reference

- **Trend is king.** Always classify trend vs TR first. With-trend entries = highest probability.
- **Trader's Equation:** prob × reward > (1 − prob) × risk.
- **Two-legged principle:** market tries to do something twice.
- **The close matters most.** Every bar, every time frame. Don't enter before the bar closes.
- **Weak signals in strong trends = FEATURE**, not a warning.
- **Most climaxes → trading ranges**, not reversals.

---

## 1. The Spectrum of Price Action

The market exists on a spectrum from extreme trends to extreme trading ranges. A trend bar and a doji bar are the two extremes of a single bar; trends and trading ranges are the two extremes of the market. Any chart segment can be classified as trending (one side dominant) or two-sided (alternating control).

**Market inertia.** Markets resist change. Almost every reversal attempt in a strong trend ends up as a flag, and the trend resumes. Successive flags get larger as bulls take profits at new highs and bears become more aggressive. Eventually the bears overwhelm the bulls and a trading range breaks to the downside — but this usually happens after several failed reversal attempts.

**Always-in direction.** If forced to be long or short right now, which would you choose? Most traders should only trade in the always-in direction. A strong trend bar can flip the always-in direction in a single bar.

**Directional probability.** During most of the day, every market has a 50–50 directional probability of an equidistant move up or down. This is the hallmark of a trading range. During a strong trend, it might be 60 or even 70 percent, but that cannot last long because it will gravitate toward uncertainty.

## 2. Bar Anatomy

| Type | Definition | Key |
|------|-----------|-----|
| **Trend bar** | Body ≥60% range | Strong = 70%+, small tails, close in top/bottom 25% |
| **Doji** | Body <40% range | Equilibrium, indecision |
| **Reversal bar** | Tail 1/3–1/2 of bar + body in reversal direction | On strong trend days: best-looking reversal bars are **countertrend traps** |
| **Outside bar** | High > prior high AND low < prior low | Close direction = bias (in TR) |
| **Inside bar** | High AND low inside prior bar | ii/iii = coiling; ioi = breakout pending |

**Signal bar** = last bar of setup (identified after close). **Entry bar** = bar where order fills (1 tick beyond signal bar). **Follow-through bar** = bar after entry — strength matters. **The CLOSE matters most.**

### Signal Bar Types

**Reversal bar (bull):** Open near/below prior close, close above open AND above prior close, lower tail 1/3–1/2 bar height, small upper tail, little overlap with prior bars, strong entry bar follows, close reverses closes/highs of more than one bar.

**Reversal bar (bear):** Open near/above prior close, close well below prior close, upper tail 1/3–1/2 bar height, small lower tail, little overlap with prior bars, strong entry bar follows, close reverses closes/lows of more than one bar.

**Two-bar reversal:** Consecutive trend bars of ~equal size in opposite directions. One of the most common and important reversal setups. Strongest moves usually begin with a reversal bar or two-bar reversal. Every reversal is some type of two-bar reversal on a smaller time frame.

**Three-bar reversal:** Variation where middle bar is small/doji. Corresponds to a 15-min reversal bar when the third bar closes at the same time as the 15-min bar.

**ii pattern:** Two consecutive inside bars, second inside the first. Coiling/breakout mode. Countertrend breakout from ii after protracted move often leads to large reversal. Stop = beyond opposite side of both bars.

**ioi pattern:** Inside-outside-inside. Breakout mode — enter in direction of breakout of second inside bar, if context supports.

**Small bars:** A small bar near the high/low of a big bar or TR can set up a reversal, especially if body is in trade direction. In a trend, small bars in pullbacks are with-trend setups only.

**Shaved bar:** No tail at one or both extremes. Setup only in strong trend.

**Exhaustion bar:** Unusually large trend bar in trend direction — can represent emotional exhaustion. All trend bars are simultaneously spikes, breakouts, and climaxes; context determines which property is dominant.

### Signal Bar Rules

- A reversal bar that largely overlaps prior bars is part of a trading range — nothing to reverse.
- A large doji is a one-bar trading range — don't buy at top or sell at bottom.
- In a strong trend, reversal bars often fail in final seconds — trapped traders fuel continuation.
- The bar after the signal bar matters: a doji inside bar = lack of urgency, consider not taking the trade.
- Doji bars are rarely good signal bars — they're one-bar TRs. Exception: near TR day extremes or with-trend in strong trend.

### Outside Bars

Every outside bar is part of a TR by definition. Usually best NOT to enter on breakout — risk too large.

**Valid outside bar entries:**
- Bar before it was a good signal bar (outside bar acts as entry bar).
- Second entry after trend line break or trend channel line overshoot.
- ioi pattern at swing extreme with small inside bar near outside bar's high/low.

**With-trend outside bars in first leg of reversal:** Function like strong trend bars. Trapped traders create powerful moves. Two legs expected from the bottom of the outside bar.

## 3. Bar Counting: H1/H2/H3/H4 / L1/L2/L3/L4

Pullback counting within a single leg. Resets on new leg.

| Count | Meaning | Reliability | Action |
|-------|---------|-------------|--------|
| H1/L1 | First pullback | Lower | Early entry in spike phase only |
| **H2/L2** | **Second pullback** | **Standard** | **Primary with-trend entry (M2B/M2S near 20-EMA)** |
| H3/L3 | Third push | Wedge potential | Often exhaustion — potential fade |
| H4/L4 | Fourth push | Context-dependent | Possible reversal |

**H2/L2 = ABC correction.** First leg = A, the H1/L1 = B, final leg = C. Breakout from C is the H2/L2 entry bar.

**H3/L3 = wedge flag.** When a bear pullback ends after a third leg, it's a low 3 sell setup = wedge bear flag. Same for bull side.

**Edge cases:**
- Low 1 in strong bull trend → NOT VALID
- Failed H2 → look for H3 as wedge top
- Context > count number always
- H2 in trend = continuation. H2 in range = reversal. Context is everything.

## 4. The Importance of the Close

**Never enter before the bar closes.** Once or twice every day, the signal you thought was forming collapses in the final seconds. You need ~8 early entries to work for every 1 that fails.

**Common traps:**
- **Trapped in:** Bull reversal bar forming nicely in bear trend, collapses to close on its low in final seconds. Early longs lose 8+ ticks.
- **Trapped out:** Trade has 3-5 ticks profit, you tighten stop on forming reversal bar, market ticks your stop then reverses back.

**The close is what institutions trade on.** Many use line charts (based on close only). The close is the only price they consider.

**Pay attention to strong closes.** If the entry bar's body suddenly increases in final seconds, reduce scalp portion, swing more contracts.

## 5. Two Legs

The market regularly tries to do something twice. All moves tend to subdivide into two smaller moves. If it fails in two attempts, it usually tries the opposite.

**Every trend line break** is the start of a new leg in the opposite direction. Any breakout, major reversal, or capitulation will usually have at least a two-legged move.

**Three-push patterns overlap with two legs.** Sometimes one leg of a two-push move is composed of two smaller legs, creating three pushes total.

## 6. Breakout Framework

Every breakout — whether successful or failed — is eventually followed by a trading range. A trend bar should be thought of as a type of gap.

**Breakout entry methods (worst → best):**
1. Breakout entry (1 tick beyond swing H/L) — worst R:R
2. Market entry on close of breakout bar — risky
3. **Pullback entry (H1/H2 or L1/L2) — BEST**
4. If ≥3 strong trend bars: buy small pullback immediately

**Breakout signals — will succeed vs fail:**

| Will Succeed | Will Fail |
|-------------|-----------|
| Large body, small/no tails | Small body, large tail |
| Breaks multiple resistance levels | Next bar: reversal/inside bar |
| Urgency, 2-3 follow-through bars | Pullbacks >⅔ of bar height while forming |
| Spike grows 5-10 bars, micro gaps | Pullback falls below BO point |
| First pullback after ≥3 BO bars, brief | Spike breaks resistance by 1 tick then reverses |

**Real breakouts feel unclear and scary.** Easy setups → small fast scalps.

**Most breakouts fail.** A breakout followed by a reversal within a few bars is common. If the reversal bar is strong compared to breakout bar → failed BO likely. If reversal bar is weak → breakout pullback likely.

## 7. 20-bar EMA

- Default moving average on ALL timeframes.
- Strong bull: price stays above, pullback to EMA = M2B entry.
- **MA gap bar:** bar not touching EMA → first one = setup for test of extreme.
- **20 gap bars:** 20+ consecutive bars not touching MA → strong trend → first touch = setup for test of extreme.
- M2B / M2S = H2 / L2 at EMA.
- If market has not pulled back to MA in 20+ bars: buy with limit order at MA, scale in lower.

## 8. Decision Vocabulary

| Decision | Meaning | Conditions |
|----------|---------|------------|
| **LONG (↑)** | Enter long | With-trend setup, signal bar, trend aligned |
| **SHORT (↓)** | Enter short | With-trend setup, signal bar, trend aligned |
| **WAIT** | Watching zone | Not at entry yet, note exact level |
| **FORGET** | Skip entirely | Low prob, unclear structure, no R:R |
| **WATCH** | Sideways but interesting | Monitor for catalyst or breakout |

**Terminology:**
- LONG = bullish bet (price UP). SHORT = bearish bet (price DOWN).
- Do NOT use "BUY"/"SELL" — always LONG/SHORT.
- Do NOT say "SHORT scenario" when meaning "sell existing long" — that is not SHORT.

### Mandatory Call Format

```
TICKER — LONG/SHORT
Entry zone: xxx – xxx
SL / Invalidation: below/above xxx
TP1: xxx  (R:R ~x:x)
TP2: xxx  (R:R ~x:x)
TP3: xxx  (R:R ~x:x)
```

## 9. Risk & Trade Management

### Stop Placement

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Initial stop** | 1 tick beyond signal bar extreme |
| **After strong entry bar** | Tighten to 1 tick beyond entry bar |
| **Doji entry bar** | Keep original stop (doji = one-bar TR, don't exit below TR in bull trend) |
| **Large signal bar** | Money management stop (e.g., 8 ticks on Emini 5-min) or ~70% of signal bar height |
| **Very large bars** | Reduce position size; risk about height of bar |

### Trailing Stops in Trends

- Trail below most recent swing low (bull) / above most recent swing high (bear).
- Move to breakeven after market moves ~halfway to profit target.
- When trend transitions to TR → stop trailing, take partials at range extremes instead.
- **Don't tighten to below a doji entry bar** — 50/50 it gets hit.

### Wide Stops

- Some traders use wide stops (e.g., 5 points in Emini) and enter when they think trend is resuming, accepting early entries.
- Wide stop + with-trend = probability ≥60%, reward ≈ risk → positive trader's equation.
- As market moves in your direction, tighten stop to reduce risk.
- Stop size is often set by the first trade of the day — note how many ticks market went against that first winner.

### Profit Taking

- **Scalpers:** OCO orders — protective stop + profit target limit. Check account after every entry/exit.
- **Swinging in strong trend:** Take ~half off at 2× initial risk. Trail stop on remainder. Take another quarter at 3× risk. Let last quarter run until clear reversal or close.
- **Swinging in TR:** Take profits at opposite side of range. Don't rely on trailing stops — pullbacks will swing below prior lows.
- **Reward often equals risk:** Market frequently rewards as many ticks of profit as it forces you to risk. Place limit order 1-2 ticks short of risk amount.
- **Profit target = flip side of protective stop.** Forces you to take profits while trader's equation is still positive.
- **Don't change plans mid-trade.** Swing traders who scalp out after losses miss big wins. Scalpers who swing after watching a trend get stopped out on next trade.

### Scaling

- **Scaling out:** Half at 2× risk, quarter at 3× risk, let rest run. Or scalp portion + swing portion with BE stop.
- **Scaling in (with-trend only):** Small position on first entry, add if market continues against you and premise is still valid. Never add to losing countertrend position.
- If market makes second move against you (e.g., low 2 in bear when long) → exit or reverse.
- Must have a plan before entering: know final stop, average entry price, total risk.

### General Rules

- **Always have a protective stop working in the market**, not just in your head.
- If your premise is no longer valid, exit — even with a loss.
- **Trader's Equation changes every tick.** If still favorable but weaker → tighten stop or take smaller profit. If marginal → exit ASAP. If negative → exit at market immediately.
- Halfway to target → breakeven stop. **TR: do NOT trail** — take partials.

## 10. Entering on Stops

**Entering on a stop is the single most reliable entry approach.** The market is moving in your direction as you enter, increasing chances of a profitable trade. Beginners should restrict themselves to stop entries until consistently profitable.

### How It Works
- Place buy stop 1 tick above signal bar high (or sell stop 1 tick below signal bar low).
- Entry bar = bar where order fills. After it closes: if strong body → tighten stop to 1 tick beyond entry bar. If doji → keep stop above signal bar.
- **Scalping math:** In Eminis, usually need a 6-tick move beyond signal bar to net a 4-tick scalp (1 tick for entry beyond + 4 ticks profit + 1 tick for limit fill beyond).

### When to Use Stops vs Limits
| Situation | Entry |
|-----------|-------|
| Strong spike | Stop (direction) or Market |
| Channel (early, 1-10 bars) | Stop on H1/H2/L1/L2 pullbacks |
| Channel (maturing, 10+ bars) | Limit (fade extremes) or Stop (pullbacks) |
| Channel (late, 20+ bars) | Limit opposite (take profits, fade) |
| TR day | Limit (buy low, sell high) |
| Pullback in trend | Stop (confirmation) |
| Beginners | Stop only |

## 11. Entering on Limits

Limit entries = betting that a move will fail and reverse. More setups than stop entries (~2:1), but riskier and harder to take emotionally. **Until consistently profitable, use stops only.**

### Key Limit Entry Scenarios

**In strong spikes:**
- Buy at close of prior strong bull trend bar in a strong bull spike.
- Buy at/below low of prior bar in strong bull spike.
- Sell at close of prior strong bear trend bar in strong bear spike.
- Sell at/above high of prior bar in strong bear spike.

**At BO points:**
- Buy just above high of bar before bull BO bar (anticipating measuring gap).
- Sell just below low of bar before bear BO bar.

**In channels:**
- Buy below low of prior bar in bull channel (early stages).
- Sell above high of prior bar in bear channel (early stages).
- Late-stage bull channel: sell above high of prior bar and above recent swing high.
- Late-stage bear channel: buy below low of prior bar and below recent swing low.

**At climaxes:**
- Buy at close of large bear trend bar at likely sell climax, also below its low and at close of next bar.
- Sell at close of large bull trend bar at likely buy climax, also above its high and at close of next bar.

**In TRs:**
- Buy at/below prior swing low at bottom of range.
- Sell at/above prior swing high at top of range.

**In pullbacks:**
- Buy on pullback to steeply rising MA, scale in at intervals below.
- Sell on pullback to steeply falling MA, scale in at intervals above.
- Buy a 60-70% pullback from current high, risking to lower low (reward ≈ 2× risk, prob ≈ 60%).

**Fading failed patterns:**
- In strong bull trend, fade short scalps — buy on limit 2-3 ticks above where short scalpers take profit.
- In strong bear trend, fade long scalps — sell on limit 2-3 ticks below where long scalpers take profit.
- Buy below low 1/2 weak signal bar at bottom of TR or new bull trend after strong reversal.
- Sell above high 1/2 weak signal bar at top of TR or new bear trend after strong reversal.

### General Rules
- A strong spike alone is NOT enough reason to enter on limit — consider context (could be exhaustive climax).
- **In strong bull trend:** low 1 and low 2 signals don't exist — they are only traps. Buy below them instead.
- **In strong bear trend:** high 1 and high 2 signals don't exist — sell above them.

## 12. Scalp vs Swing

| Type | Hold | Profit | Prob | When |
|------|------|--------|------|------|
| Scalp | 1-15 min | ≈ risk | 67%+ needed | Obvious, high-prob |
| Swing | 15 min–full day | ≥ 2× risk | 40-50% | Unclear/scary |

Obvious → scalp. Unclear/scary → swing. Best: scalp part + swing balance with BE stop.

---

## 13. Need Two Reasons

Always have ≥2 reasons before entering.

**Valid:** Signal bar, MA pullback (H2/L2), BO pullback, pullback in always-in market, S/R test, dueling lines, H2/L2 in context, DB/DT, wedge/final flag, any failure, second entry.

**One reason only:** (1) Strong trend (2) TL overshoot + good reversal bar (3) Second entry.

---

## 14. Trader's Equation

> `prob × reward > (1 − prob) × risk`

| Win Rate | Min Reward | Setups |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| 70%+ | ≥ 0.5× risk | High-prob scalps (rare) |
| 60%+ | ≥ risk (prefer 2×) | Pullbacks in trends, BO pullbacks |
| ~50% | ≥ 2× risk | TR entries, fading BOs |
| ≤40% | ≥ 2-3× risk | Reversal tops/bottoms |

Confident → 60%. Uncertain → 50% — don't take unless reward >> risk. **Never** reward < risk unless 80%+ win rate.

## 15. ETFs and Inverse Charts

**SPY** ≈ Emini chart, sometimes clearer due to smaller tick size. **SDS** (UltraShort S&P 500, 2× inverse) — if a bull flag on Emini looks like a rounding bottom on SDS, don't buy the flag.

**Triple witching days:** SPY has larger gap opening than Emini due to price adjustment, but trades tick-for-tick after that.
