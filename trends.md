# Trends — Al Brooks Framework

> Al Brooks, "Trading Price Action Trends" (2011, Wiley). Load ONLY when market is trending or trend is developing. For universal concepts (bar anatomy, bar counting, breakouts, risk management), see `core.md`. Brooks terminology only.

---

## 1. Trend Classification

| Type | Structure | MA Alignment | Bars | Pullbacks |
|------|-----------|-------------|------|-----------|
| **Bull trend** | HH + HL | 20-EMA > SMA50, price above 20-EMA | Majority bull trend bars (body ≥60%) | Shallow, 1-2 legs |
| **Bear trend** | LH + LL | Reverse | Majority bear trend bars | Shallow |
| **Trading range** | Overlapping | 20-EMA flat | Alternating direction | N/A — fade extremes |

**Trend is a series of trending swing highs and lows.** Once established, the most profitable trades are in the trend line direction until the trend line breaks.

## 2. Signs of Strength Checklist

### 22 Signs of a Strong Trend

| # | Sign |
|---|------|
| 1 | Big gap opening at day start |
| 2 | Trending highs AND lows |
| 3 | Majority bars = trend bars in trend direction |
| 4 | Little/no overlap of consecutive bar bodies |
| 5 | Bars with no/small tails (urgency) |
| 6 | Gaps between bodies (bull: open > prior close) |
| 7 | Breakout gap: strong trend bar at trend start |
| 8 | Measuring gap: pullback doesn't drop below breakout bar high |
| 9 | Micro measuring gap: low after strong bull bar ≥ high of prior bar |
| 10 | No big climaxes |
| 11 | Countertrend bars are LARGEST bars (traps) |
| 12 | Countertrend setups look BETTER than with-trend (feature) |
| 13 | No significant trend channel overshoots |
| 14 | Corrections go SIDEWAYS, not countertrend |
| 15 | Failed wedges and failed reversals common |
| 16 | MA gap bar sequence (20+ bars away from 20-EMA) |
| 17 | Few-to-no profitable countertrend trades |
| 18 | Small, infrequent, sideways pullbacks |
| 19 | Sense of urgency — pullback never comes |
| 20 | Trending closes, highs, lows, or bodies |
| 21 | No two consecutive closes on opposite side of MA |
| 22 | Trend breaks multiple resistance levels by many ticks |

≥12 signs → strong trend → with-trend only.

> **"Once you realize strong trend, you don't need a setup to enter."** — Brooks

### Strong Breakout Signs

- Large trend body, small/no tails. Larger bar = more likely to succeed.
- Spike goes very far, lasts several bars, breaks several resistance levels (MA, prior swing H/L, trend lines) by many ticks.
- 5–10 bars without pulling back more than a bar or so.
- Bars with lows at or 1 tick below prior close (bull) / highs at or above (bear).
- Bars with opens above prior close (bull) / below (bear).
- Bars with closes on/near their extremes.
- First pullback only after 3+ bars, lasts 1-2 bars, doesn't reach breakout point.
- Breakout reverses many recent closes and highs/lows (5–20+ bars).

### Strong Reversal Signs

- Strong reversal bar with large body, small/no tails.
- Next 2–3 bars also trend bodies ≥ average size.
- Spike grows to 5–10 bars, reverses many bars/swing H/L/flags of prior trend.
- Second attempt to reverse (second signal).
- Reversal from trend channel line overshoot.
- Prior breaks of opposite trend lines (not first sign of strength).
- Pullback to test extreme lacks momentum (overlapping bars).
- Pullback fails at MA or old trend line.

## 3. Entry Types for Trends

| Type | Entry | Stop | Target |
|------|-------|------|--------|
| **H2/L2 at 20-EMA** | 1 tick beyond signal bar | 1 tick beyond signal bar extreme | Measured move of prior leg |
| **Spike & channel** | 1 tick above/below channel line break | Opposite side of channel | Scalp + trail |
| **1st breakout pullback** | 1 tick beyond signal bar | 1 tick beyond signal bar | Measured move of breakout |
| **Climactic reversal (2-stage)** | 1 tick beyond pause bar after climax | Beyond climax extreme | Measured move + prior swing |
| **Late entry** | At market (swing portion only) | Same stop as original entry | Same target as original |
| **Second entry** | Same as first, 2nd attempt | Same as first | Same as first (more reliable) |

**Countertrend entries** (lower probability): only when second signal, climax + opposite breakout, or failed wedge. Generally avoid.

### How to Trade a Trend

When in a trend, look for ANY reason to enter with-trend. The simple existence of a trend is reason enough to enter at least a small position at market.

**Stop entry approaches:**
- H2 pullback to MA (bull) / L2 pullback to MA (bear)
- Wedge flag pullback
- Breakout pullback after flag breakout
- H1 in strong spike (not after climax)
- Buy above prior swing high in very strong bull trend
- Sell below prior swing low in very strong bear trend

**Limit entry approaches (experienced):**
- Buy at bull trend line or prior swing low (potential double bottom bull flag)
- Sell at bear trend line or prior swing high (potential double top bear flag)
- Buy at/below weak low 1 or 2 signal in possible new bull trend
- Buy at/below low of prior bar in quiet bull flag at MA
- In strong trend: buy at market, at close of every bull trend bar, or on limit at/below low of prior bar

**Trade management in trends:**
- Trail stop below most recent swing low (bull) / above swing high (bear).
- Raise to breakeven after pullback tests entry then new high.
- Take partial profits at 2× initial risk minimum.
- Hold swing portion until clear reversal or close.
- Don't change plan mid-trade: swing traders who scalp out miss big wins.

## 4. Spike & Channel

```
Spike: ████ ████ ████    → strong, one-sided, momentum
Channel: ██ ██ ██        → two-sided, tails bigger, overlapping
TR: ════ ════            → trading range forms
```

- **Spike phase:** aggressive entries, no pullback needed. 1–5 strong trend bars.
- **Channel phase:** all bars = potential with-trend setups. Slower, two-sided.
- **After spike & channel** → usually TR → double bottom/top pattern.
- **Every trend bar is a climax or part of one.** Climax ends with pause/reversal bar, NOT necessarily opposite trend.

**Spike & channel is the fundamental market structure.** It happens to some extent every day. The start of the channel usually becomes the start of an incipient trading range. Bottom of channel phase gets tested within a day or two. Bears scale into shorts during channel; bulls return to buy at channel bottom → TR broadens.

**All bull channels are functionally bear flags.** All bear channels are functionally bull flags. If the trend is strong, breakout may go sideways and trend continues. Rarely, trend accelerates out of channel — usually climactic, fails within 5 bars.

## 5. Trend Lines

**Bull trend line:** Drawn across lows. **Bear trend line:** Drawn across highs.

**Primary use:** Entries in trend direction on pullbacks. After break, look for opposite-direction entries after test of extreme.

**Drawing:** Swing points or best fit. Can use candle bodies only (common in wedges). Usually erase after verification.

**Key rules:**
- Once trend established by trending H/L, most profitable trades are in trend line direction until break.
- Every pullback to trend line area (even undershoot/overshoot) → look for reversal → enter with trend.
- After break, trend extreme usually gets tested.
- **Trend line break = first sign market no longer controlled by one side.**
- Each successive trend line has flatter slope → losing momentum.
- If repeatedly tested in small number of bars: either breaks through (reversal) or accelerates away.
- Strength of break indicates strength of countertrend traders.

**Most breakouts fail!** Strong sell-offs to bull trend line look like reversals — beginners short, experienced traders buy with limit orders at/below the line.

## 6. Trend Channel Lines

**In a bull channel:** trend line below lows, trend channel line above highs. **In a bear channel:** reversed. Same general slope.

**Primary use:** Fading a trend that has gone too far, too fast. Look for overshoot that reverses, especially second penetration.

**Drawing:** As parallel of trend line (drag to opposite side), across swing points on opposite side, or best fit.

**Wedge = convergent channel.** When trend line slope > trend channel line slope, urgency is building. Bulls buying on smaller pullbacks, bears exiting on smaller sell-offs. Creates wedge → reversal setup.

**Why overshoots cause reversals:** Last shorts give up and cover → spike up → euphoric bulls buy → not enough shorts left → trapped longs panic → one-sided selling. Even if narrative is imprecise, pattern is entrenched in institutional psyche.

**Smart traders won't trade countertrend until:** pullback from strong trend line break, OR reversal from trend channel line overshoot.

## 7. Channels

**A channel is a sloping trading range.** Bull channel = bear flag. Bear channel = bull flag.

**Triangles are channels:** Expanding (two diverging lines), contracting (two converging lines), ascending (resistance + bull trend line), descending (support + bear trend line). Wedge = convergent variant.

**Vacuum effect:** Market races to top/bottom of channel because traders hold back, expecting the line to be reached. Creates sharp moves to extremes that attract naïve traders → institutions fade.

**Channel lifecycle:**
1. At start: trade with trend (buy below lows in bull channel).
2. As two-sided trading develops (overlapping bars, bear bodies, tails): experienced traders start fading.
3. Channels usually have at least 3 legs before ending.
4. Breakout → measured move target = height of channel.
5. Most breakout attempts from channels fail.

**Strong channels (steep, tight, little overlap):** Risky to trade first breakout against trend. Wait for breakout pullback. If rally above MA and first pullback stays above MA → strong. If pullback below MA → weaker.

---

## 8. Micro Channels

**Definition:** Trend line drawn across 2–10 bars where most bars touch or are close, with trend channel line on opposite side. Extremely tight — no pullbacks or rare, small pullbacks.

**Strength:** More bars, stronger trend bodies, smaller tails → stronger → first pullback more likely to fail to reverse trend.

**Trading micro channels:**
- **With-trend entry on failed breakout:** Bull micro channel in bull trend, first bar that pokes below micro trend line = high 1 buy setup. Enter 1 tick above that bar's high.
- **Don't enter against:** Bear micro channel in bull trend at MA = bull flag. Don't short. Buy the failure instead.
- **Only use for with-trend setups.** Micro trend line shorts in bull trends near MA are losing trades.
- **10+ bar micro channel** = unsustainable climax → tradable reversal likely.

---

## 9. Horizontal Lines: Swing Points

**On TR days:** Horizontal lines across prior swing H/L often serve as barriers → failed breakouts → reversals. Fade of second HH or LL = even more likely (second signal).

**On trend days:** Use horizontal lines only to enter on pullbacks. After BO of TR, pullback to BO level + reversal = breakout pullback entry.

**Don't fade strong trends.** Only consider fading swing H/L in trends if there was first a good trend line break AND a strong reversal bar. Even then, only scalp.

## 10. Pattern Evolution

> "Reliable patterns fail about 40% of the time and often evolve."

| Original | Evolves Into |
|----------|-------------|
| Failed L2 short | Wedge top |
| Failed final bear flag | Breakout pullback sell → larger TR |
| Spike & channel | TR → double bottom bull flag |
| Double top bear flag | Double bottom bull flag (opening hour) |
| Expanding triangle 5 legs | 7 legs |
| Micro TL breakout fails | Breakout pullback |

**Agent rule:** Setup failed? Check for trapped traders → fuel for opposite move. After many sideways bars → new pattern is independent.

## 11. Day Type Classification

| Day Type | Characteristics | Trading Approach |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Strong Trend / Trend from Open** | Direction from bar 1, small pullbacks | All with-trend signals, enter at market. Best reversal bars = traps. |
| **Trending TR** | Opening range 1/3–1/2 avg range, breakout after 1-2h | Both directions, swing with-trend bias |
| **Reversal Day** | Trends one way → reverses toward close | Second entry after reversal bar. Strong last-2h reversal = follow-through next day. |
| **Trend Resumption** | Strong trend → tight TR → resumption | Anticipate resumption, enter at first pullback. Leg 2 ≈ Leg 1. |
| **Stairs / Broad Channel** | 3+ trending swings, each pullback overlaps prior BO | With-trend entries only, expect next-day continuation. Shrinking = reversal likely. |

### Reversal Day

- Most start as trending trading range days.
- If reversal starts in last couple of hours and is strong → follow-through next day and often several days.
- Some strongest trends begin mid-day or end of day as TR breakouts or climactic reversals.
- Restrict trading to new direction once recognized.

### Trend Resumption Day

- Strong trend first hour → TR for hours → trend resumes final 1-2 hours.
- TR is often very tight — don't give up on boring midday.
- Often a brief 1-2 bar breakout that fails between 11am-noon PST (trap), followed by real BO in trend direction.
- Can span multiple days: strong move → TR 1-2 days → resume.
- Second leg often ≈ first leg in size.

### Stairs / Broad Channel

- At least 3 trending swings resembling mildly sloping TR/channel.
- Each BO followed by pullback beyond BO point (overlap between swings).
- Both bulls and bears active, can trade both directions, but swing with-trend.
- **Shrinking stairs:** Each BO smaller than prior → waning momentum → deeper pullback or reversal likely.
- Pay attention to how many ticks BOs run past recent swing → use that to fade subsequent BOs.
